From Hair Waste To High-Tech: Turning Salon Scraps Into Sunscreen.

About Us

Hair is a rich source of keratin, a protein, and melanin, the pigment responsible for hair color. Researchers extract these compounds through a process involving heat, hydrochloric acid, and chemical dialysis. Melanin, in particular, has natural UV-blocking properties, making it a potential sunscreen ingredient. This approach offers a double benefit: Reduces waste: Hair scraps from salons would be diverted from landfills and put to good use. Creates eco-friendly sunscreens: Hair-derived melanin could potentially lead to more sustainable sun protection products. It's still under development, but this research holds promise for the future of waste management and sun protection.
